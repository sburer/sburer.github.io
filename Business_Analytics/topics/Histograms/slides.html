Histograms

  • See this YouTube tutorial to construct histograms of the various types discussed here

  • A histogram is a graphical summary of a single column/variable of data
  • Purpose is to show the data’s “look and feel”
  • Depicts the frequencies (or counts) of data in various bins
  • Most often depicted as a bar chart

Source: gist.github.com

  • To create a histogram in Excel, use a PivotTable with bar chart

  • Example: Excel file homes.xlsx

  • Often will need to group data into bins manually

  • To force the display of empty bins, first click a cell in the rows of the PivotTable and then use the PivotTable’s Field Settings > Layout & Print

Cumulative Histograms

A cumulative histogram is a variation of the regular histogram

  • For ratio/numerical data
  • The cumulative frequency for a bin is the sum of all frequencies at or below that bin
  • Can be depicted on top of a histogram using a line graph

Example: Excel file homes.xlsx

Relative histograms and cumulative relative histograms are further variations

  • Based on relative frequencies, i.e., percentage of data in bins
    • Divide frequencies by total number of pieces of data
  • Shows the exact same info, just on a standardized scale
  • Can be created directly from frequency info
